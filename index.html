/ About Me
Sophomore @ Northeastern University

Markets, data, and long-term thinking.

I'm Jeremy, a sophomore with a deep passion for finance and investing. Ever since I read my first annual report, I was hooked on the idea that behind every stock ticker is a real business, with real people, real products, and a real story worth understanding.

My investing philosophy is rooted in fundamentals. I believe in doing the homework: reading 10-Ks, understanding competitive moats, and thinking in decades rather than quarters. I'm particularly interested in value investing, inspired by the principles of Benjamin Graham and Warren Buffett, but I also keep a close eye on macro trends and how interest rates, inflation, and global events reshape the opportunity landscape.

Beyond stocks, I'm fascinated by the intersection of data science and finance, how quantitative models, machine learning, and alternative data are changing the way professionals think about risk and return. My coursework in data science has given me tools to explore these questions hands-on, from building valuation models in Python to visualizing market trends with D3.js.

Outside of markets, I enjoy reading economic history, following Fed policy decisions, and debating investment theses with friends. I believe that financial literacy is one of the most powerful skills a young person can build and I'm still early in the journey.
